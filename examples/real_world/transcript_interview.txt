The speaker explains that a reliable knowledge workflow starts by confirming
primary material before analysis.

They warn that metadata, thumbnails, and page titles can help route a task, but
they cannot prove what a speaker actually said.

The recommended workflow is to preserve the transcript, split it into evidence
segments, map claims to source text, and only then write a report.

The speaker adds that useful reports should label source facts, cautious
inferences, and extensions separately so readers can audit the conclusion.
