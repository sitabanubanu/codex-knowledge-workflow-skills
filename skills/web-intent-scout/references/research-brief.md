# Research Brief

Use this after intent mapping and before searching.

## Core Rule

Lock the research target before collecting evidence.

The brief prevents the agent from searching broadly, following SEO rankings, or answering a different question than the user's real decision.

## Brief Shape

```markdown
Research Brief:
- User decision:
- Core questions:
- Must-verify facts:
- Required source types:
- Boundaries:
- Clarification need:
- Success condition:
```

## Core Questions

Generate 3-7 questions. They should be answerable by evidence, not personality or taste alone.

Good questions:

- What current official source confirms the price, policy, version, or availability?
- What independent evidence supports or contradicts the vendor claim?
- What risks would change the recommendation?
- What region, platform, language, or budget constraint matters?
- What would make this candidate unsuitable?

Weak questions:

- Is this good?
- What is popular?
- What do people think?
- Which one is best?

## Must-Verify Facts

List facts that cannot be assumed:

- price and limits;
- current version or release date;
- jurisdiction or region;
- privacy/data handling;
- refund/cancellation;
- compatibility;
- official support;
- safety, legal, financial, or medical constraints.

## Boundaries

State what not to over-answer.

Examples:

- Do not rank tools globally; rank for the user's stated workflow.
- Do not infer legal advice from blog summaries.
- Do not treat old reviews as current product behavior.
- Do not recommend paid products without checking pricing and refund risk.

## Clarification Rule

Ask before searching only when the answer branch would materially change:

- region or jurisdiction;
- budget;
- platform;
- language;
- risk tolerance;
- task goal.

If the user wants speed, state assumptions and search multiple likely branches.
