# Report Routing

Use this before deciding the final report shape.

## Core Rule

Report shape follows evidence center of gravity.

Do not force every person into the same final structure.

## Routing Signals

Read the handoff, overview, evidence ledger, and user goal. Identify:

- what topic occupies the most evidence;
- what topic has the strongest emotional or practical stakes;
- what topic the user explicitly asked about;
- what topic has uncertain but report-changing claims;
- what topic is low-weight and should be moved to appendix.

## Common Routes

| Evidence Center | Emphasize | Keep Short |
|---|---|---|
| Relationship / romance | approach and withdrawal, conflict loops, standards, attachment signals, relationship timeline | generic career or abstract values sections |
| Work / study | ability structure, execution model, constraints, growth strategy, decision environment | romance speculation unless evidence supports it |
| AI / creation / projects | creation thread, tool use, product sense, engineering habits, output path | generic personality labels |
| Family / origin | family role, responsibility boundary, long-term influence, current correction | unsupported clinical language |
| Friendship / group chat | group role, social function, interaction rhythm, relationship network | totalizing private-life claims |
| Emotion / mental-health signals | triggers, regulation, protective factors, limits | diagnosis in main report |
| Mixed | multi-line model, weighted sections, appendices | one grand explanation |

## Dynamic Structure

A final report may use different first-level questions depending on route.

Relationship-heavy examples:

- What relationship pattern keeps repeating?
- When does the person move closer, and when do they withdraw?
- What kind of response do they actually need?
- What relationship evidence is too weak to conclude from?

Work/study-heavy examples:

- What ability structure is most supported?
- What environment triggers execution?
- Where does long-term action break?
- What realistic path follows from the evidence?

Creation/project-heavy examples:

- What is the current creation or product line?
- Is this a tool, interest, identity, or career path?
- What converts thought into output?
- What risks turn exploration into drift?

## Placement Rule

Weight must affect:

- section order;
- section length;
- tone strength;
- whether the item is in main report or appendix.

Low-weight material should not receive a full main section simply because it is interesting.
