# Learning Article Template

Adapt wording and section names to the user's language, but preserve these
functions:

1. State what is worth learning.
2. State the source's core question and thesis.
3. Show a compact knowledge map.
4. Teach prerequisites before dependent concepts.
5. Explain each selected concept: meaning, importance, relationship, and evidence.
6. Reconstruct the source's development without turning timestamps into headings.
7. Explain each important example: what it is, why it appears, how it works, and what it supports.
8. Separate transferable Inference or Extension from Source content.
9. Give a learning order, skip-for-now boundary, first action, and focused check.
10. Disclose evidence limits and provide an original-source location index.

When the pack records `source_reanalysis.mode: evidence_bound`, disclose near
the opening that the semantic inventory was reconstructed from admitted source
material. Keep each reconstructed Source row's support rationale visible or
auditable; do not present reanalysis as an ordinary upstream extraction result.

Reject articles that merely list topics, timestamps, claims, or abstract labels.
Do not use phrases such as “底层逻辑”“认知升级” or “更深层变化” unless the
article defines the change and connects it to source evidence.

Keep the prose natural. Source / Inference / Extension boundaries may appear in
framing and a closing section instead of interrupting every sentence.
